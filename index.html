Step 1: Business Areas capture the process requests

• Business areas will capture their process requests on the content management application.
• The requests come to our process backlog for prioritization.

Step 2: Backlog grooming session prep

• Determine team capacity and utilization for upcoming sprint.
• Backlog grooming session is booked, and all interested stakeholders are socialized.

Step 3: Internal prioritization by interested stakeholders

Interested stakeholders will earmark their priority processes ahead of the backlog session.

Step 4: Facilitation of Backlog grooming session

* Updates are given regarding previous sprint (performance outcomes, learnings and continuous improvement opportunities)
* Priority of process is determined by the ABC stakeholders using the RISE framework to determine the process intake for the upcoming sprint.
* Processes to be mapped are agreed upon with ABC stakeholders for the upcoming sprint

Step 5: Sprint commences

Analysts will complete processes using the following RAG statuses:
* Client engagement
* Process walkthrough
* Process mapping
* Work instructions
* Business signoff
N.B., Business sign off is the target of the sprint as Quality Assurance & Repository Capture are external to the BizX team.

Step 6: Sprint concludes

* Analysts submits processes to QA.
* Once QA is completed with checks (possible reworks) the process is then submitted to the Repository Capture team (if applicable) to capture on the Repository Capture platform.
* Once Repository Capture is completed, the process is published
* The QA and Repository Capture elements are variable regarding timelines due to the respective capabilities being external
